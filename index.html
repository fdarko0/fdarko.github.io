Francis Diawuo Darko, PhD

Environmental Social Scientist | Climate, Mobility & Wellbeing

About

I am an environmental social scientist and human geographer specializing in migration, environmental change, and wellbeing in sub‑Saharan Africa. My research examines how structural inequalities shape mobility, adaptation, and social inclusion.

Research

  • Climate change and mobility
  • Environmental governance and inequality
  • Wellbeing, ageing, and migration
  • Indigenous knowledge and sustainability

Current Work

I am currently developing research on constrained mobility and ageing, examining how environmental stress and structural inequalities shape long-term wellbeing outcomes.

Publications

Selected work includes publications in Development in Practice, Migration Studies, and GeoJournal.

Teaching

I have taught across universities in Australia and Ghana in environmental studies, migration, climate change, and research methods, emphasizing student-centered and applied learning.
